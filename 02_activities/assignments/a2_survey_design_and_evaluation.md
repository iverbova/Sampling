## Part B - Survey Evaluation:

Identify and describe survey features:

```
            1. **Sample type**  
            The 2018 GSS GVP used a **probability sample** with **stratified sampling** across 27 strata based on province and metropolitan area. Within each stratum, a **simple random sample** of records (groupings of telephone numbers) was drawn.

            2. **Sample size**  
            The target sample size was **20,000** respondents. The final sample, after rejective sampling and exclusions, consisted of **16,149 respondents**.

            3. **Target population**  
            Individuals **15 years of age and older** living in private households in the **ten Canadian provinces**. Residents of the **territories**, **full-time institutional residents**, and those without telephone access were excluded.

            4. **Sampling frame**  
            Constructed from **landline and cellular telephone numbers** linked to addresses using Statistics Canada’s **Address Register (AR)** and additional sources (e.g., Census data). Both AR-linked and unlinked telephone numbers were included.

            5. **Survey mode(s)**  
            Data were collected through:
            - **Self-completed electronic questionnaires (rEQ)**
            - **Computer-assisted telephone interviews (iEQ)**

            6. **Timeline**  
            Data collection occurred from **September 4 to December 28, 2018**.

            7. **Response rate**  
            The overall response rate was **41.9%**, which included both modes of response.

            8. **Weights**  
            The survey used **person-level weights (WGHT_PER)** that adjusted for:
            - Probability of selection
            - Non-response (complete, item, and partial)
            - Rejective sampling (non-volunteers)
            - Calibration to population benchmarks (e.g., age, sex, income, geography)

            9. **Data processing**  
            Included:
            - Automated and manual **edit checks**
            - **Coding** of open responses using standard classifications (e.g., NAICS, NOC, ICNPO)
            - **Imputation** for item non-response
            - **Derivation** of additional analytic variables

            10. **Cleaning, imputation, etc.**  
            - **Deterministic edits** and **flow editing**
            - Imputation for donation amounts and demographic items
            - Special processing (e.g., donation perturbation and rounding) for confidentiality

            11. **Sources of error**  
            - **Sampling error** from using a probability sample
            - **Non-sampling errors**, including:
                - Non-response bias
                - Measurement error (e.g., recall bias)
                - Processing and coding errors

            12. **Limitations, known biases, etc.**  
            - **Exclusion** of territories and institutionalized populations
            - **Under-coverage** of households without phones
            - **Mode effects** due to mixed-mode design (online vs phone)
            - Comparability to previous cycles is limited due to **methodological changes** (e.g., introduction of online mode)

            13. **Link to documentation and any additional sources used**  
            [General Social Survey – Public Use Microdata Files](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/45-25-0001/index-eng.htm)
            [2018 GSS GVP User Guide (PDF)](./Sampling/02_activities/assignments/2018_GSS33GVP_PUMFGuide.pdf)
 ```
